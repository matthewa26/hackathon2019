Introducing the 2017 Order of the Arrow Hackathon

By Chuck Coutteau

Are you an Arrowman interested in computers, web development, or technology? Then you will probably be interested in the 2017 Order of the Arrow Hackathon taking place in sunny California this upcoming October. But what exactly is a Hackathon?

Choose your path...

Novice

“Novice” delegates will focus on experiences and learning new skills, both in technical and in leadership aspects. They will compete in an innovative display and have the opportunity to earn merit badges and possible requirements for Supernova awards. No experience necessary!

Intermediate

“Intermediate” delegates will spend half of the program gaining similar skills and then will compete against each other in a typical hackathon. They will be given 24 hours to solve a problem through technological efforts to mitigate a real-world issue within our Order. These Arrowmen should have some exposure to computer programming before arrival.

Advanced

“Advanced” delegates will have the option to work together in teams made up of Arrowmen with varying technical knowledge to brainstorm and develop solutions to prompts. This will be the more typical hackathon program, spending most of the program creating these solutions. Arrowmen in this track should know how to code in multiple programming languages.
